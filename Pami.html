About Palouse Advocates on Mental Illness

Palouse Advocates on Mental Illness is an informal support group for families of people with mental illness, as well as those carrying a diagnosis themselves. We firmly believe that treatment can help people manage their illness and lead to better function and stronger relationships. An important portion of our meetings is checking in with each other.

Back in 1981, our Non-Profit Organization realized that by working together we could overcome our challenges much more efficiently, and that is why we ultimately decided to begin this support group.

Major Mental Disorders Present

The major mental illnesses our members or their family members have had experience with include:

  • Depression
  • Bipolar Disorder
  • Schizoaffective Disorder
  • General Anxiety Disorder
  • Schizophrenia Disorder
  • Panic Disorder
  • Post Traumatic-Stress Disorder
  • Suicidal thinking and actions
  • Dual Diagnosis - cooccurring mental illness and substance abuse
